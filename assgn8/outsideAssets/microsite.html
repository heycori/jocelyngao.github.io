HCII MICROSITE


CONCEPTS: TYPOGRAPHY, GRIDS, INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE
SKILLS: TYPOGRAPHIC SIGNALS, GRID DESIGN, WIREFRAMING, UI PROTOTYPING
TOOLS: INVISION, FIGMA

Digesting large amounts of text is extremely difficult if the text is not designed well. Through color manipulation and specific typography choices, designers can communicate hierarchy of information while making the entire comprehension process more enjoyable.

​ The goal of this project was to introduce the fundamentals of design through assignments that build on one another, eventually leading to the creation of a microsite that applies the concepts and skills learned through readings and assignments.

​ Throughout the process, I sketched different combinations of typographic signals and grids that would act as the base of my UI prototype, and later incorporated color once the skeleton was decided. For the color palette of my microsite, I chose to use purple as the central hue that my monochromatic color theme would use, while keeping a dark background to give the microsite a sharp look. LEARNING PROCESS


TYPOGRAPHY



The typographic assignment was comprised of three parts, each building on the previous part by introducing an additional typographic signal to better communicate the information to readers.

In my first iteration, I chose incorporate the typographic signal line spacing because it allowed me to cluster relevant information together, and increasing the line spacing allowed for a more “breathable” read of the information.




I then incorporated size as my second signal since it was a great way to emphasize and bring attention to the more important and relevant information, and since it also allowed for the creation of headers and sub-headers.




Finally, I added shift in my final iteration because creating horizontal differences allowed me to further split up the information based on content similarities, and it gave the layout another dimension.





INTRODUCING GRIDS, COLOR, AND IMAGES















​ COLOR CHOICE

​ After learning about colors and their characteristics, I chose to use the two primary colors red and yellow in lieu with CMU’s red and a celebratory gold. I used red to highlight all information relevant to CMU and their celebration of 25 years, and light yellow for the rest.

​ I also wanted to use warm colors to give off a feeling of happiness and energy for the celebration of 25 years, passion for the HCII and all its impactful work, and power for the domination that this newer department will continue to have moving forward. Within the warm colors, I also wanted to create a stark contrast between the two by choosing colors with a huge difference in values so that I could effectively emphasize certain aspects of the text.


​ ITERATIONS

​ In the first (top) design, I chose to emphasize the dates over the information about the event, but the information was too dispersed, hurting the readability.

​ To fix this, in the second design, I tried to create a more common and consistent read (left to right, top-down), and emphasized the purpose of the event more by creating negative space, while still using size to emphasize the dates of the event.

​ In my third design, I kept a similar structure to the second design, but created a lot of negative space on the left half of the page to create novelty.

​ In my first incorporation of an image (fourth design), I used a similar format to my second design, but created room for the image by shifting the information in the middle to the right. However, I wasn’t visually pleased with this design, leading to my final design at the bottom where I filled up the negative space I created in my third design with an image that fit my color theme, and “pointed” to the information on the right.
