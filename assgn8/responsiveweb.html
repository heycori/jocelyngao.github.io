DESIGN PROCESS


RESEARCH



CUSTOMER JOURNEY MAPS

Individually,our team conducted secondary research on the emergency response area, and narrowed our scope to focus on the EMS Team at Carnegie Mellon University. Then our team created a script to interview a total of 9 people to create 3 personas: user, service provider, and business owner.

From the interviews, we created a customer journey map for the two ends of an EMS call: the service provider and the user, and identified pain points and opportunities for both.









PERSONAS

After collecting and analyzing the information we found, we were able to create three personas that our responsive web would be designed to benefit: EMS Chief, EMS volunteer, and the caller.










SPEED DATING RESULTS

Each team member generated 3-5 scenarios of how the responsive website could help a persona. With a total of 15 scenarios, our team voted on the top 5 scenarios, and identified the needs satisfied in each. Then we each created 2 storyboards, each satisfying a different need, and speed-dated them for feedback.







What we found was that areas with the most opportunities were reducing anxiety during wait times and educating students about EMS services prior to an emergency. Seeing that the pain point was more severe for wait times, our final decision was to design an interface that included a tracker, next recommendations, and contact options for improving wait time experiences for both EMS team members and callers.

INDVIDUAL HI-FIDELITY MOCK-UPS

Once the wire-frames and flows were decided, each team member individually designed hi-fidelity prototypes of the same two screens.





COMBINING FOR A FINAL DESIGN
